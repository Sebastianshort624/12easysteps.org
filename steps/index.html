1

We Admitted We Were Powerless

over our substance or compulsive behavior — that our lives had become unmanageable

Step One — The Diagnosis

Joe McQ and Charlie P. explain Step One as the essential diagnosis — the foundational "problem statement" of the entire recovery process. Before any solution can be applied, the person in recovery must fully understand and concede to their innermost Person 7 the exact nature of the problem.

Step One is not a casual admission. It is a profound, ego-shattering realization that human willpower, intelligence, and Person 7-will are insufficient to solve this problem alone. Joe and Charlie argue that without a deep, honest understanding of this diagnosis, no subsequent step will take hold.

The Two-Part Problem

  • Part One — The Physical Allergy (The Body): Once a person introduces our substance or compulsive behavior into their system, an abnormal physical reaction is triggered. This creates a "phenomenon of craving" — a compulsion that makes it physically impossible to stop once started. This is not a moral failing; it is a biological reality.
  • Part Two — The Mental Obsession (The Mind): The mind produces an "insanity" — a thought so powerful it overcomes all reasons not to engage. The mind convinces the person that this time it will be different; that they can control it; that they need it to cope. This mental blank spot is what makes the problem Person 7-defeating.
  • Total Powerlessness: Because the body cannot handle it AND the mind cannot stay away from it, the person is rendered absolutely powerless. Neither part alone would be fatal — together they form an inescapable trap that no human power can unlock.

Joe & Charlie — What They Teach

  • "Many try to 'mess around' before finally, truly meaning it when they say they are through."
  • Step One requires surrendering Person 7-propulsion, Person 7-direction, and Person 7-reliance — all three must go.
  • It is a "conclusion of the mind" — not an emotional breakdown, but a clear-eyed rational acceptance of a fact.
  • The "gift of desperation" — a willingness to do anything — only comes when a person has truly hit their bottom with Step One.
  • Without a genuinely convinced Step One, Step Two makes no sense and Steps Three through Nine cannot be sustained.

Part One — Powerlessness Over our substance or compulsive behavior

The Big Book states: "We admitted we were powerless over our substance or compulsive behavior." Powerlessness means that once the cycle begins, the person cannot reliably stop it. This is not about willpower — many people in recovery have enormous willpower in other areas of life. This is a specific allergic reaction.

The sponsor's role here is to listen carefully, evaluate whether the evidence is specific and personal rather than vague or theoretical, and to be genuinely convinced that the sponsee understands — not just says the words.

Sponsee Evidence — Powerlessness

Sponsee writes
Describe specific times when you started using or engaging in our substance or compulsive behavior and could not stop, even when you wanted to. What happened once you started? What does this tell you about your power over it?
The sponsor is looking for specific personal evidence — not a theory. Vague statements like "I lost control sometimes" are not enough. Real dates, real situations, real consequences.

Sponsor — Assess This Evidence

Part Two — Life Has Become Unmanageable

Unmanageability is the second half of Step One. This goes beyond the behavior itPerson 7 — it encompasses the entirety of life. Relationships, finances, mental and emotional health, work, spiritual life — all become distorted, unstable, or destroyed as a result of the obsession and its consequences.

Joe and Charlie emphasize that the word "unmanageable" means the person cannot manage their own life because of the mental obsession and the behaviors it produces. It is not that life is merely difficult — it is that the person lacks the internal architecture to run their own life without help from a Power greater than themselves.

Sponsee Evidence — Unmanageability

Sponsee writes
What specific areas of your life have become unmanageable because of our substance or compulsive behavior? Describe consequences in relationships, finances, mental health, work, family, and spirituality. Be honest and thorough.
The sponsor is looking for evidence that multiple areas of life have been affected — not just one incident. Real consequences, not hypothetical ones.

Sponsor — Assess This Evidence

The Doctor's Opinion — The Allergy

The Big Book opens with "The Doctor's Opinion," written by Dr. William D. Silkworth. He introduces the concept of an allergy of the body combined with an obsession of the mind. The allergy is not a metaphor — it describes an abnormal physical reaction to our substance or compulsive behavior that people without this condition simply do not have.

In an allergy, the body reacts abnormally to a substance that others can tolerate. For one who has this allergy, the reaction does not diminish with time — it progresses. The phenomenon of craving, once triggered, cannot be stopped by willpower, moral conviction, or even the sincere desire to stop.

  • The body has the allergy — it produces a craving upon introduction of the substance
  • The mind has the obsession — it repeatedly generates the idea to introduce the substance again
  • Together, they form a cycle that human power alone cannot break
  • This is why "white-knuckling" and willpower-based recovery approaches consistently fail for the person who truly has this condition

Sponsee Evidence — The Allergy Pattern

Sponsee writes
Have you noticed that once you introduce our substance or compulsive behavior, you cannot stop? And that your mind keeps returning to it even when you want to stay away? Describe this pattern in your own experience. How is your reaction different from someone without this allergy?

Sponsor — Assess This Evidence

The Pickle Analogy — A Teaching from the Rooms
"A cucumber can be turned into a pickle. But a pickle can never be turned back into a cucumber."
Old-Timer's saying — illustrating the permanent nature of the allergy

What This Means — Permanent Condition

The Big Book uses the language of a permanent, progressive, and incurable condition. Once a person has crossed the invisible line into our substance or compulsive behavior, the body and mind have been fundamentally changed. There is no "going back" to a time before the allergy existed.

No amount of time away from the substance, no amount of therapy, no spiritual experience reverses the physical allergy. A person who has this condition cannot safely return to "controlled" or "moderate" use — the allergy is still present, waiting to be triggered.

This is not a punishment or a moral judgment. It is simply a fact about how this particular person's body and mind are constituted. Accepting this fact — fully, deeply, without reservation — is the completion of Step One.

  • "Once an alcoholic, always an alcoholic" is not a sentence — it is a description of a physical reality
  • The person with this condition must simply never introduce the substance, because the pickle cannot become a cucumber
  • This permanent nature is what makes a Power greater than onePerson 7 necessary — not as a crutch, but as a daily operating reality

Sponsee Evidence — "This Will Always Be the Case"

Sponsee writes
What convinces you that this condition is permanent — that you cannot simply "moderate," "control," or eventually return to normal use of our substance or compulsive behavior? What evidence do you have that this will always be the case for you specifically?
This is where the sponsee moves from "I have a problem" to "I have an incurable condition that requires a permanent solution." The language matters. The conviction matters.

Sponsor — Assess This Evidence

The Sponsor's Experience — Sharing Step One

The sponsor now shares their own experience with Step One. This is not a lecture or a critique of the sponsee's evidence — it is the sharing of one person's journey with another. It demonstrates that the sponsor has genuinely lived through this step and found it to be true.

The sponsor's share grounds the process in relationship and human connection rather than just intellectual exercise. It says: "I have been where you are. This step is real. It worked for me. I am convinced it will work for you."

Sponsor's Share — Step One Experience

Sponsor writes
Share your own experience with Step One. How did you come to be fully convinced of your powerlessness and unmanageability? What evidence did you have? What did it take for you to truly accept the permanent nature of your condition?

Step One — Completion

The sponsor now reviews all evidence collected and confirms their conviction before graduating the sponsee to Step Two. Each box below must be checked sincerely — not as a formality.

2

Came to Believe That a Power Greater Than Ourselves

Could Restore Us to Sanity

Step Two — The Prescription

If Step One is the diagnosis, Step Two is the prescription. Joe and Charlie describe the transition from Step One to Step Two as the move from a hopeless problem to a hopeful solution. Step One left us "absolutely licked" — Step Two offers the first glimmer of a way out.

"Sanity" here does not mean clinical mental health. It refers specifically to the mental obsession — the insane idea that we can use our substance or engage in our behavior and get a different result. Step Two asks simply: can we believe that something — anything — greater than our own thinking can restore us to clear, rational thought?

Joe and Charlie emphasize that Step Two requires only willingness to believe — not deep faith, not certainty, not a specific religious tradition. The Big Book says "We found that Person 8 does not make too hard terms with those who seek Him." The only requirement is an open mind and a willingness to try.

The Second Step Proposition — Page 53

The Big Book states: "Either Person 8 is everything or else Person 8 is nothing. What was our choice to be?" Joe and Charlie teach that this is the pivotal intellectual question of Step Two. It forces a binary choice and eliminates the comfortable middle ground of a vague "sort of" belief.

  • If human power was sufficient, Step One would not be true — but we have already proven it is
  • Therefore, something beyond human power must be available
  • Step Two does not require the sponsee to find "a Person 8 in the sky" — it requires only a willingness to believe that a Power exists that can do what human power cannot
  • This Power is often first experienced in the Fellowship itPerson 7, or in the sponsor relationship

Sponsee Evidence — Belief in a Power Greater Than YourPerson 7

Sponsee writes
Have you come to believe — or are you at least willing to believe — that a Power greater than your own thinking and willpower exists and can restore you to sanity? What experiences, observations, or moments of willingness bring you to this belief?

Sponsor — Assess This Evidence

Sponsor's Share — Step Two Experience

Sponsor writes
Share your own path through Step Two. How did you come to believe — or come to be willing to believe — in a Power greater than yourPerson 7? What doubts did you have? What shifted?

Step Two — Completion

Confirm your conviction before graduating the sponsee to Step Three.

3

Made a Decision to Turn Our Will and Our Lives

Over to the Care of Person 8 as We Understood Person 8

Step Three — A Decision, Not a Transaction

Joe and Charlie emphasize that Step Three is a decision — an act of will, made once, but practiced continuously. It is not a one-time event that permanently solves all problems. It is a commitment to stop running one's life on Person 7-will and to allow Person 8's will to be the operating principle.

The Big Book compares it to a businessman who has been running a company into the ground. At some point, the partners intervene and say: "Let's hand this over to the care of a Greater Manager." The businessman doesn't disappear — they continue to work — but they no longer run the company on Person 7-propulsion.

The phrase "as we understood Person 8" is critical. Step Three does not impose any specific religious tradition. The only requirement is honesty about the Person 8 of one's own understanding — which often begins simply as "Not me."

Sponsee Evidence — Making the Decision

Sponsee writes
Describe what it means to you to "turn your will and your life over to the care of Person 8 as you understand Person 8." What are you willing to turn over? What is difficult about this decision? Are you willing to make this decision today?
"Person 8, I offer myPerson 7 to Thee — to build with me and to do with me as Thou wilt. Relieve me of the bondage of Person 7, that I may better do Thy will. Take away my difficulties, that victory over them may bear witness to those I would help of Thy Power, Thy Love, and Thy Way of life. May I do Thy will always."
The Third Step Prayer — Big Book, p. 63
The Big Book instructs: "We thought well before taking this step, making sure we were ready; that we could at last abandon ourselves utterly to Person 8." Joe and Charlie note that this prayer is meant to be said with a sponsor or trusted person — it is not a solo act. The decision is witnessed, not merely private.— Alcoholics Anonymous, p. 63
