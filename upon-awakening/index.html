A Practice for Ordinary Days

Upon Awakening

Work through each prompt in your own words, right on this page. Fill it out fresh each day, then print or save a copy once it's done.

01 Look back on yesterday. Is there anything you owe an apology or amends for, and what's one corrective step you could take?
02 Think about the 24 hours ahead. What do you plan to do, and how do you want to move through it?
03 Check your motives for today's plans. Is anything driven by self-pity, dishonesty, or looking out only for yourself?
04 Is there a decision you can't quite settle right now? Describe it, and what's making it hard to land on an answer.
05 This kind of clarity tends to build slowly, not all at once. Where do you need to be patient with yourself as you practice this?
06 What's one thing today you could approach by asking to be shown the next step, instead of mapping out every detail yourself?
07 Is there something you want today that's really just for you? Who else, if anyone, does it serve?
08 Is there someone — a partner, friend, or mentor — you could invite into this practice with you today, even briefly?
09 Where do you feel agitation or doubt today? What response would actually help, rather than just relieve the feeling?
10 Notice any self-pity or resentment showing up. Name it plainly — what's it actually about?
11 Beyond fear — is excitement or anger running the show anywhere today? What would handing over the wheel look like there?
12 Name a fear or worry you're carrying. What would it look like to set down the need to control it?
13 Where are you tempted to force an outcome today? What would it mean to ease off instead?
14 Looking back on the day: what went well, what didn't, and what's worth adjusting tomorrow?
15 Reflection on its own isn't enough. What's one concrete action you'll actually take on what came up today?
Fill in each field, then use "Print this page" — your answers print exactly as written. Nothing is saved once you close the page.
